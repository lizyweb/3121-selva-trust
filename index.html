About us

SELVA Trust has been working with the community in several platforms since 1997. The Managing Trustee, Ms. R Vasuki is an active and enthusiastic social worker and is a pillar of strength for the entire village and its neighbourhood. People approach her for any of their problems and she provides solutions or at least directs them to the right place for solution.

SELVA Trust worked in Nagapattinam during Tsunami rehabilitation. They found a unique solution to the problem faced by women from the fishermen community. They were unable to get any space in the market or seashore to sell their fish. The men managed to occupy all prime slots. They trained 200 women to ride bicycles. The bicycles were supported by the project donor. With the help of these bicycles, the women rode directly to the villages/hamlets for door-to-door sale of fish.

Profile of the Chief Functionary

Ms. Vasuki the chief facilitator of this organization had been worked with a NGO Pudukottai district since 1992 till 1996. Mrs. VASUKI, the grassroots social worker and women activist, along with likeminded women social workers, and grassroots groups leaders in the villages SELVA TRUST established in the year 1996, registered under Indian Trusts Act of 1882 in the year 1996 on 31-12- 1996Ms. Vasuki, an experienced woman social worker who worked with leading NGOs in Pudukottai district as Coordinator for 6 years and gained experience in mobilization of women, encouraging them to small savings, internal lending, building the capacities of the women grassroots, and networking them during her tenure as women coordinator. She has under gone training in women empowerment, sustainable agriculture, and alternative livelihood for the poor households. She worked with the NGOs for 7 years since 1992.

She underwent 10 days training with NATURE TRUST, Thiruvarangulam in 1998. Then she undergone 1 year with NATURE Trust in 1999 in herbals, medicinal plants and home medicine production. She had attended the HIV/AIDS International Conference- 5 days in 1993, in Chennai hosted by Dr. MGR Medical University, Chennai. She underwent 7 days training in reproductive and child health organized by TNVHA, Chennai in 2000.She has been facilitating the network to address the girls and women in exploitation under Sumangali scheme in Pudukkottai district in partnership with Center for World Solidarity, Secunderabad for the past 7 years successfully. In Pudukkottai, SELVA Trust has been working with single women for the past 18 years. The women are given training to identify small enterprises through which they can earn their livelihood with dignity. They are then being supported with Seed money support to start their activity.

Programs

WELFARE PROGRAMS

Inclusive Self-Help Group Initiatives

"Strengthening Communities through Inclusive Self-Help Group Initiatives" emphasizes the importance of fostering unity and self-reliance among diverse groups within society. By promoting self-help groups for women, men, children, and persons with disabilities, this initiative aims to empower individuals to work together, share resources, and improve their quality of life.

Savings and Credit Linkages

"Financial Empowerment through Savings and Credit Linkages" focuses on building economic resilience within communities. By encouraging small savings, internal lending, and creating credit linkages with banks, individuals and self-help groups gain access to financial resources. This system enables them to manage their finances better, invest in livelihood opportunities, and enhance their economic stability.

Income Initiatives for Women and Landless Laborers

"Income Generation Initiatives for Women and Landless Laborers" aims to empower vulnerable groups by providing them with opportunities to enhance their livelihoods. Through various training programs and support systems, women and landless laborers can develop skills and engage in income-generating activities. These initiatives not only promote financial independence but also foster self-esteem and community cohesion, enabling participants to contribute positively to their families and local economies.

Tsunami Relief and Rehabilitation for Women Fish Vendors in Kodiayakarai

"Tsunami Relief and Rehabilitation for Women Fish Vendors in Kodiayakarai" focuses on providing essential support and recovery resources to women affected by the tsunami disaster. By addressing the unique challenges faced by head-loading fish vendors in the region, this initiative aims to restore their livelihoods and promote economic resilience. Through targeted relief efforts, training programs, and access to financial resources, these women can rebuild their businesses and contribute to the community's overall recovery and growth.

"Comprehensive Care and Support for People Living with HIV/AIDS"

"Comprehensive Care and Support for People Living with HIV/AIDS" emphasizes the importance of holistic health services and emotional support for individuals affected by the virus. This initiative aims to provide medical care, counseling, and educational resources to empower patients and reduce stigma. By fostering a supportive community environment, it encourages individuals to seek treatment, adhere to medication, and improve their quality of life. Ultimately, this approach promotes awareness and understanding, contributing to the overall well-being of those living with HIV/AIDS.

Marginal Women Farmers through Sustainable Agriculture

"Empowering Marginal Women Farmers through Sustainable Agriculture" focuses on enhancing the livelihoods of women farmers who often face economic and social challenges. By promoting sustainable agricultural practices, this initiative aims to equip them with the knowledge and tools needed to improve crop yields, conserve resources, and protect the environment. Through training, access to markets, and support networks, these women can achieve greater financial independence and contribute to their families and communities, fostering resilience and sustainability in agriculture.

Environment and ecology conservation activities

"Promoting Environment and Ecology Conservation Initiatives" aims to safeguard natural resources and preserve biodiversity for future generations. Through community engagement and awareness campaigns, this initiative encourages sustainable practices that protect ecosystems and mitigate environmental degradation. By fostering collaboration among local stakeholders, it seeks to implement conservation strategies that balance ecological health with community needs, promoting a harmonious relationship between people and nature. Ultimately, these efforts contribute to a more sustainable and resilient environment.

Women Marginal Farmers through Sustainable Horticulture in Pudukkottai

The initiative for sustainable horticulture among women marginal farmers in the Kothamalagam area of Pudukkottai district, in partnership with CWS, Secunderabad, aims to enhance agricultural practices and improve livelihoods over three years. By providing training, resources, and support, the program empowers women farmers to adopt eco-friendly horticultural techniques that increase productivity and resilience. This collaboration not only boosts their economic independence but also promotes sustainable farming practices that benefit the local ecosystem. Ultimately, the initiative seeks to create a lasting impact on the community's agricultural landscape and the well-being of its farmers.

TB Awareness and Prevention Initiatives in Partnership Program

The initiative for TB awareness, prevention education, and follow-up interventions in partnership with REACH, Akasaya Program in Chennai aims to combat tuberculosis through comprehensive community engagement. This program focuses on educating the public about TB prevention and treatment options while providing essential resources for early detection and care. By facilitating ongoing support and follow-up interventions, it seeks to improve health outcomes for affected individuals and reduce the spread of the disease. Ultimately, this collaborative effort aims to foster a healthier community and raise awareness about the importance of TB control.

Tamil Nadu Bio diversity & Greening Project

The Tamil Nadu Biodiversity & Greening Project, in collaboration with JICA (Japan International Cooperation Agency), focuses on conserving the rich biodiversity of Tamil Nadu while promoting sustainable development. This initiative aims to enhance ecological health through reforestation, habitat restoration, and the protection of endangered species. By engaging local communities and stakeholders, the project fosters environmental awareness and encourages practices that support biodiversity conservation. Ultimately, it strives to create a greener and more sustainable future for the region.

Livelihood Improvement Project in Pudukkottai District

The "Empowering Single Women: Livelihood Improvement Project in Pudukkottai District" focuses on enhancing the economic status and quality of life for 1,000 single women across five villages. Through targeted training, skill development, and access to resources, the project aims to provide these women with sustainable livelihood opportunities. By fostering independence and resilience, the initiative empowers participants to build better futures for themselves and their families. Ultimately, this project seeks to create a supportive community that uplifts and advocates for the rights of single women.
